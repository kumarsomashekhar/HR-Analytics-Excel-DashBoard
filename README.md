# HR Analytics Dashboard Project Report

## Project Objective
Analyze employee attrition, 
workforce demographics, 
education, departments, 
and job roles using an HR Analytics Dashboard to support data-driven HR decisions.

## Dataset Overview
[Dataset](https://github.com/kumarsomashekhar/HR-Analytics-Excel-DashBoard/blob/main/HR%20DATA_Excel.xlsx%20-%20Data.csv)
The dataset contains 1,470 employee records with demographic, education, department, job role, and attrition information.

### Executive Summary
Total Employees: 1470
Active Employees: 1233
Attrition Count: 237
Attrition Rate: 16.12%
Average Age: 37
Job Satisfaction Rating: 2.6

### Gender Analysis
Male: 882 (60%)
Female: 588 (40%)
The workforce is predominantly male.

### Education-wise Attrition
Bachelor's: 99
Master's: 58
Associate: 44
High School: 31
Doctoral: 5
Bachelor's degree holders show the highest attrition.

### Department-wise Attrition
R&D: 56.12%
Sales: 38.82%
HR: 5.06%
R&D contributes the largest share of attrition.

### Job Role Analysis
Laboratory Technician: 62
Sales Executive: 57
Research Scientist: 47
Sales Representative: 33
These roles experience the highest turnover.

### Age Analysis
25–34: 112
35–44: 51
Under 25: 38
45–54: 25
Over 55: 11
Employees aged 25–34 show the highest attrition.

### Marital Status Analysis
Married: 673
Single: 470
Divorced: 327

## Recommendations
Focus retention efforts on R&D and Sales. Improve career growth programs for employees aged 25–34 and Bachelor's degree holders.
## Business Impact
Reducing attrition can lower hiring costs, improve productivity, and retain organizational knowledge.
