# HR Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard developed in Power BI to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, education background, department performance, job roles, and employee engagement metrics.

The dashboard helps HR professionals and business leaders identify workforce trends, understand key attrition drivers, and implement data-driven retention strategies.

---

## Project Objective

The primary objective of this project is to:

- Analyze employee attrition patterns across departments and job roles.
- Identify demographic groups with higher turnover rates.
- Evaluate the impact of education, age, gender, and marital status on attrition.
- Provide actionable insights to improve employee retention and workforce planning.
- Enable decision-makers to monitor key HR metrics through an interactive dashboard.

## Key Business Insights

### Workforce Composition
- The organization consists of 1,470 employees.
- Male employees represent 60% of the workforce, while females account for 40%.

### Attrition Trends
- The attrition rate stands at 16.12%, indicating a notable employee turnover level.
- R&D contributes more than half of total attrition, making it the most affected department.

### Education Analysis
- Employees with Bachelor's Degrees exhibit the highest attrition count.
- Doctoral Degree holders show the lowest attrition, suggesting stronger retention among highly specialized employees.

### Job Role Analysis
- Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives experience the highest turnover.
- Technical and customer-facing roles appear more vulnerable to attrition.

### Age Analysis
- Employees aged 25–34 represent the largest attrition segment.
- Early-career professionals are more likely to leave compared to older age groups.

### Employee Engagement
- The Job Satisfaction Rating of 2.6 suggests opportunities to improve employee engagement and workplace satisfaction.

---

## Business Recommendations

### Improve Employee Retention
- Focus retention initiatives on R&D and Sales departments.
- Conduct periodic employee engagement surveys.

### Career Development Programs
- Provide structured learning paths and promotion opportunities.
- Develop leadership training programs for high-potential employees.

### Target High-Risk Employee Segments
- Create retention strategies for employees aged 25–34.
- Strengthen mentorship and career guidance programs.

### Enhance Job Satisfaction
- Review compensation and benefits.
- Improve recognition and reward systems.
- Promote work-life balance initiatives.
